# Day 31 — Portfolio Architecture: Building Your Proof System
 
**Date:** 2026-06-13
 
---
 
## What I Did
 
Researched 10 portfolios of employed security analysts and engineers before designing my own. Documented what separated the strong ones from the weak ones, then designed the full architecture — site map, narrative arc, 5 hero projects, and homepage copy. No building yet — design first.
 
---
 
## What I Concluded
 
The best security portfolios don't show everything. They show the right things, explained well. A portfolio with 20 shallow projects is worse than one with 5 deep ones because it signals someone who collects experiences instead of learning from them. Curation is a skill. It tells a recruiter that you understand what matters and what doesn't.
 
The other thing that struck me — the portfolios that felt most compelling were the ones that showed process, not just outcomes. Screenshots of a completed challenge prove you finished it. A write-up that explains what you were thinking, where you got stuck, and what you'd change proves you actually understood it. That's a much stronger signal.
 
My portfolio needs to answer one question in under 30 seconds: can this person actually do the work? Everything else is noise.
