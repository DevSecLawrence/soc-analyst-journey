# Day 30 — Month 1 Complete
 
**Date:** 2026-06-12
 
---
 
## What I Concluded
 
One month of focused, daily work produces more than I expected and less than I hoped. More — because I can now investigate real attack chains, write detection rules from scratch, and produce professional reports. Less — because the skills matrix showed me exactly how many gaps still exist between where I am and where I need to be.
 
The most important thing Month 1 taught me is that documentation is not separate from the work — it IS the work. The GitHub repo isn't evidence of what I learned. It's how I learned it. Writing up every day forced me to articulate what I actually understood versus what I thought I understood. Those are very different things.
 
---
 
## Assumption I Made
 
I assumed that by Day 30 I'd feel confident. I don't feel confident. I feel capable — which is different. Confidence is "I know I can do this." Capable is "I have real tools and real experience and I can figure out what I don't know yet." I think capable is actually more honest and more useful. Overconfident analysts miss things. Capable analysts know their limits.
 
---

## Uncertainty I Have
 
I don't know if Month 1 is enough to get noticed. The GitHub is real, the work is documented, the investigations are legitimate — but I have no external signal yet that any of it translates to opportunity. No recruiter has reached out. No interview. I'm 30 days in and still operating entirely on faith that consistency compounds. I believe it does. I haven't seen proof yet.
 
---
 
## What I Would Tell Day 1 Me
 
Don't try to learn everything. Pick a direction — blue team, SOC, detection — and go deep in that one direction for the whole month. The temptation to explore everything is real and it's a trap. Depth in one area is worth more than surface knowledge across five.
 
And build in public from Day 1. Not when you feel ready. Not when you have something impressive to show. From Day 1. The discomfort of showing incomplete work is exactly the accountability mechanism that keeps you showing up. Use it.
