# Day 28 — Self Assessment: What I Actually Know After Month 1
 
**Date:** 2026-06-10
 
---
 
## What I Concluded
 
A month ago I didn't know what a Sigma rule was. I couldn't read a PCAP file. I'd never opened Event Viewer with any real intention. Now I've investigated real attack chains, written detection rules from scratch, scored 10/10 on a phishing analysis challenge, and written professional incident reports for three different audiences.
 
That's real progress. But the skills matrix I did today showed me something important — knowing a concept and being able to use it under pressure in a real SOC environment are two completely different things. Most of my ratings sit at 3 or 4. Comfortable in a lab. Not battle-tested.
 
The gap that hit hardest: EDR platforms. 7 out of 10 job descriptions mentioned CrowdStrike, Carbon Black, or SentinelOne and I have zero hands-on experience with any of them. A lot of real SOC work happens in an EDR, not just a SIEM. That's something I didn't fully appreciate before today.
 
The thing I'm most confident in: detection engineering. YARA rules, Sigma rules, phishing IOC extraction — this is where my work is strongest and most documented. If someone asked me to walk through how I built a YARA rule and why, I could do that right now.
 
---

## Assumption I Made
 
I assumed that doing the labs was enough. It's not — at least not for every skill. Some things like log analysis and phishing investigation I've practiced enough that I'd be comfortable in an interview. But other things like cloud security and EDR platforms I've barely touched, and just knowing they exist doesn't close the gap. Month 2 needs to be more deliberate about which skills I'm actually building versus just reading about.
 
---
 
## Uncertainty I Have
 
I don't know if my self-ratings are accurate. I might be overconfident on some things and underconfident on others. The only real test is doing actual work — not labs, not challenges, but real incidents in a real environment. I don't have that yet and I won't until I get a job. So I'm working with the best information I have, which is how well I can explain things and how comfortable I feel navigating the tools.
